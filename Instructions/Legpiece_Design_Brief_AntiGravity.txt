4. Homepage Sections (in scroll order)
Each section below is described with its content, design intent, key interactions, and copy direction. Sections must flow seamlessly on mobile with no horizontal scroll.

Section 1 — Hero
DESIGN INTENT
Full-viewport hero. Appetite-first. The dish must fill the screen. CTA is the only action.

CONTENT
	•	Background: full-bleed photograph of Shahi Makhmali Legpiece Masala Curry (2-piece bowl, overhead or 3/4 angle)
	•	Overlay: brand red panel (60% opacity OR solid left-aligned panel) housing the headline
	•	Headline: “Barwani ka asli zaika, ab ghar tak” (Hindi) + “Real Nimadi flavour, delivered” (English subhead)
	•	Price anchor: “Starting ₹199” in gold/amber type
	•	CTA button: “Call to Order” — tapping dials +91 82360 95399 (tel: link)
	•	Secondary link: “See full menu ↓” smooth-scrolls to Section 4
	•	Logo top-left, phone number top-right in nav bar

MOBILE BEHAVIOUR
Hero image cropped to portrait. Overlay takes full bottom 40% of viewport. CTA button is 56px tall minimum (thumb-friendly).

Section 2 — Nimadi signature story
DESIGN INTENT
A brand-soul section. Not a product card — a story. Warm, editorial, slightly slower pace to let the reader breathe.

CONTENT
	•	Section label: “Our signature”
	•	Headline: “Shahi Makhmali Legpiece Masala Curry”
	•	Subhead: “A unique blend of purity and taste”
	•	Body (English): Two large juicy chicken leg pieces marinated overnight in fresh onions, yogurt, and ginger-garlic paste. Slow-cooked with hand-ground spices and premium dry fruits until the gravy is thick and velvety. Experience the authentic desi flavour in every bite.
	•	Body (Hindi): (Bilingual block — right column or below, lighter weight, same copy in Devanagari)
	•	Price badge: ₹399 / 2 legpiece
	•	Dish image: right-aligned or full-width cinematic crop

NOTES FOR ANTI GRAVITY
This section should feel like a premium restaurant’s ‘About the dish’ page. Not a product card. Use generous whitespace and let the image breathe.

Section 5 — Suggest a dish
DESIGN INTENT
Community-engagement section. Encourages repeat visits and brand loyalty. The mechanic: users vote on submitted dish ideas; the week’s winner gets made and the suggester gets a free combo. This is the brand’s primary growth and retention tool on the website.

SUB-COMPONENTS
	•	Last week’s winner banner: shows winning dish name and suggester’s first name + votes received. Background: warm green. Signals that the mechanic is real.
	•	Voting list: 4 current week suggestions shown as cards with vote count, progress bar, and a ‘+ Vote’ button. One vote per user per week (session-based at minimum; phone OTP-based preferred).
	•	Suggestion form: three fields — Dish name, Short description (2 lines max), Phone number. Submit button triggers confirmation state.
	•	Fine print: voting rules, prize value (₹469 Royal Solo Combo), announcement schedule (winner every Sunday via WhatsApp).

MECHANIC RULES (FOR DEVELOPER)
	•	Voting resets Monday 12:00 AM
	•	Winner announced Sunday, pinned to top of section for the following week
	•	Phone number collected for winner contact only — no marketing use without consent
	•	Minimum backend: a simple Airtable or Google Sheets webhook is acceptable for V1

PRIZE DISPLAY
Prize callout badge at top-right of section: “Free combo for the winner” in gold/amber on dark background. Prize is specifically the Royal Solo Combo (₹469 value). Make the prize feel tangible.

6. Technical Specifications

6.1 Performance
	•	Lighthouse performance score target: 90+ on mobile
	•	Images: WebP format, lazy-loaded, max 200KB per image
	•	Fonts: system stack preferred; web fonts max 2 families, preloaded
	•	No heavy JS frameworks required — vanilla JS or lightweight library preferred for V1

6.2 Mobile-first requirements
	•	Breakpoints: 375px (small phone), 430px (large phone), 768px (tablet), 1280px (desktop)
	•	All tap targets minimum 48×48px
	•	Phone number links must use tel: protocol
	•	WhatsApp links must use wa.me/91XXXXXXXXXX format
	•	No hover-only interactions on mobile

6.3 Accessibility
	•	All images must have descriptive alt text in English
	•	Contrast ratio minimum 4.5:1 for body text
	•	Veg/non-veg dot indicators must also use text labels (not color alone)
	•	Form fields must have visible labels (not placeholder-only)

6.4 Hosting & domain
To be confirmed by client. Suggested stack: static site (Next.js or plain HTML/CSS/JS) hosted on Vercel or Netlify. Suggest-a-dish voting backend: Airtable API or Google Sheets via Apps Script for V1.

7. Copy & Tone Guidelines
The website copy should feel warm, proud, and confident — the voice of someone who genuinely believes their food is the best in Barwani (because it is). Not corporate. Not generic delivery app language.

Do
Don’t
Use “asli”, “shahi”, “desi zaika”
Say “delicious food” or “tasty dishes”
Reference Nimadi / Nimar roots specifically
Use generic “Indian cuisine”
Write prices as ₹399/- (with dash)
Write “Rs. 399” or “INR 399”
Bilingual EN + HI on dish descriptions
Translate entire UI into Hindi
Mention slow-cooking, hand-ground masala
Use vague “fresh ingredients”
Keep CTAs direct: “Call to Order”
Use “Order Now” (no online ordering exists)
